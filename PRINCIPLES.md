# Principles of the Remedial Legitimacy Framework

## What This Is

The instruments in this repository run to tens of thousands of words. They use legal language, philosophical argument, and structural analysis to make their cases. That is necessary for the instruments to function as instruments.

This document is not an instrument. This document is the version you can read in five minutes and know whether someone has crossed a line.

If any of the bullet points below describe something that is happening, the longer documents explain what to do about it. But recognizing that something is wrong should not require a law degree. Most of what follows amounts to: **don't do things to people that you would not tolerate being done to you, and don't let institutions do things to people that no person would be allowed to do.**

That's it. The rest is specifics.

---

## On Institutions and the People They Serve

1. **Institutions exist to serve people, not to perpetuate themselves.** An institution that has become primarily concerned with its own survival, funding, or authority — at the expense of the obligations it was created to fulfill — has lost the basis for the compliance it demands.

2. **Authority is conditional.** The obligation to comply with institutional authority depends on that institution performing its foundational duties. An institution that systematically violates the rights it is obligated to protect cannot simultaneously demand obedience from the people it is failing.

3. **Awareness creates responsibility.** An institution that is aware of harm being done to the people it serves and chooses not to act is participating in that harm. The same applies to individuals.

## What Is Prohibited

None of the following should require explanation. All of them apparently do.

The following behaviors, when conducted or permitted by institutions against the people they are obligated to serve, are the conditions this framework exists to address:

### Violations of Person

These should be obvious. They are listed because they keep happening.

- **Unlawful surveillance** — don't watch people without legal authority or legitimate purpose.
- **Arbitrary detention** — don't hold people without lawful basis.
- **Suppression of expression** — don't punish people for speaking, writing, organizing, or asking questions.
- **Retaliation** — don't make someone's life worse because they asserted their rights or reported misconduct.

### Failures of Justice

If you demand people follow the rules, you have to follow the rules. This is not a complex concept.

- **Denial of justice** — if someone has a legitimate complaint, hear it.
- **Delay as denial** — you cannot take seven years to process a case and then call the result "justice." Especially while demanding immediate compliance from the person waiting.
- **Selective enforcement** — same rules, same application. If the rule only gets enforced against certain people, it is not a rule. It is a weapon.
- **Prosecutorial abandonment** — you cannot vigorously prosecute minor infractions by ordinary people while systematically declining to prosecute institutional misconduct. People notice.

### Institutional Betrayals

The gap between what an institution says it does and what it actually does is the gap this entire framework lives in.

- **Saying one thing and doing another** — your mission statement is not a defense if your behavior contradicts it. Everyone can read both.
- **Capture** — if the institution is controlled by the interests it is supposed to regulate, it is not regulating. It is collaborating.
- **Weaponization of process** — bureaucratic procedures exist to serve legitimate purposes. Using them to exhaust, harass, or punish people is not a legitimate purpose. This should not need stating.
- **Destroying or suppressing evidence** — if the documentation of your conduct is so damaging that your response is to destroy it, the documentation was accurate.

### Degradation of Democratic Governance

Democracy is not a logo. It is a set of conditions. When the conditions are removed and the logo is kept, that is not democracy. It is branding.

- **Electoral manipulation** — don't suppress votes, gerrymander districts, or interfere with the peaceful transfer of power. This is the floor, not the ceiling.
- **Legislative capture** — laws should reflect the interests of the public, not the interests of whoever spent the most on lobbying. This is a simple test that a remarkable number of laws fail.
- **Erosion of judicial independence** — courts that cannot check the other branches of government are not courts. They are notaries.
- **Exclusion by design** — if ordinary people cannot understand or participate in the processes that govern their lives, those processes are not public. They are private processes with a public-facing website.

### Targeting of Individuals

This section describes what happens when the machinery described above is pointed at a specific person. Every technique listed here has documented historical precedent. None of them are hypothetical. All of them are designed so that describing them accurately makes the person describing them sound unreliable. That is the point.

- **Ambient harassment** — coordinating the degradation of a person's life through actions that are each individually deniable and collectively devastating. If no single incident is provable but the person's life is visibly falling apart, someone should ask why.
- **Credibility destruction** — engineering the conditions that make a person look unstable *before* they describe what is being done to them. The audience's skepticism arrives pre-installed. That is not a coincidence.
- **Professional interference** — damaging a person's career through channels that cannot be traced back to a source. Background checks, reference calls, anonymous tips, commercial reputation systems. Each one is a legitimate service. The pattern they form is not legitimate.
- **Social isolation** — cutting a person off from the people who would corroborate their account. A person who cannot be corroborated is a person who cannot be believed. That is the operational objective.
- **Biography fracture** — creating years in someone's life that they cannot explain to any institutional audience. The years were not wasted. They were consumed by the operation. But the explanation — "I was being targeted" — is the one explanation that the operation was specifically designed to make incredible.

### Manipulation of Populations

If you are shaping what people believe without their knowledge, you are not persuading them. You are operating on them. The distinction matters.

- **Covert preference engineering** — don't secretly shape people's political beliefs and then point to those beliefs as evidence of democratic consensus. The beliefs were manufactured. The consensus is a receipt.
- **Ambient democratic substitution** — if the formal mechanisms of democracy are preserved but the substantive content is determined by actors invisible to participants, what remains is not democracy. It is a managed outcome with a ballot-shaped interface.
- **Exploitation of susceptibility** — conducting influence operations against populations whose critical faculties have already been degraded by the commercial attention economy is not sophisticated statecraft. It is picking a fight with someone who has already been hit.

## What Is Required

Knowing something is wrong and doing nothing is a choice. The framework treats it as one. When these conditions are documented:

1. **Documentation is a duty.** Those who witness institutional failure have an obligation to record what they observe, preserve what they can, and make their testimony available.

2. **Silence is participation.** Remaining quiet about known institutional misconduct — particularly when one has the capacity to speak — is itself a form of complicity.

3. **Mutual aid is obligatory.** Signatories to the framework's instruments accept reciprocal obligations to support one another through evidence preservation, testimony, pattern correlation, and material assistance when institutions fail.

4. **Outside accountability is legitimate.** When domestic institutional mechanisms fail comprehensively, external accountability — through international mechanisms, distributed documentation networks, public advocacy, and diplomatic pressure — is not interference. It is the minimum non-complicit response.

5. **Confrontation may be warranted.** When documentation alone does not alter institutional behavior, active confrontation — through interposition, counter-documentation, non-cooperation, and delegitimation forcing — is philosophically justified as the exercise of obligations that institutional failure has activated.

## What Is Not Permitted

The framework constrains its own participants too. Accountability tools that become instruments of abuse are worse than useless — they discredit the entire project. So:

- **No new harms.** Accountability actions must not create the conditions they claim to remedy.
- **No weaponization.** The framework's instruments cannot be used for personal vendettas, commercial advantage, or purposes unrelated to institutional accountability.
- **No conscription.** Participation is voluntary. No one may be pressured, guilted, or coerced into joining, and declining to participate may not be characterized as moral failure.
- **No fabrication.** Documentation must be accurate. Evidence must be authentic. False or manipulated records are grounds for exclusion.
- **No institutional capture.** The framework's own structures must not become the self-perpetuating bureaucracies it was designed to address.

## The Core Position

This is simple enough to fit on an index card:

Institutions derive their authority from the obligations they fulfill. When an institution systematically fails those obligations — when it violates the rights it exists to protect, ignores the misconduct it exists to remedy, or serves the interests it exists to regulate — the people affected are not required to wait indefinitely for self-correction.

They are entitled to document, to witness, to organize, and to act.

The instruments in this repository provide the structure for doing so. This document provides the test for whether you need them. If you recognized something in the list above, you probably do.
