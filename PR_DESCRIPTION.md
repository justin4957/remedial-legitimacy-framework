# [DRAFT] Compact of Mutual Intervention

## Summary

This PR introduces the **Compact of Mutual Intervention**, a new distributed accountability instrument that establishes mutual assistance obligations for outside accountability when sovereignty degrades through accumulated institutional failures.

**Core Innovation**: Extends NATO Article 5's collective defense principle to sovereignty degradationâ€”systematic institutional failures affecting one group of citizens are treated as threats to the sovereignty of all citizens, justifying collective accountability response.

## Category

**B. Distributed Accountability Instruments**

## Gap/Failure Mode Addressed

**Comprehensive Domestic Accountability Failure**: When all domestic accountability mechanisms fail simultaneously through:
- Court delays rendering justice ineffective
- Prosecutorial discretion becoming systematic non-prosecution
- Legislative capture by concentrated interests
- Electoral accountability failing through manipulation
- Administrative agencies serving regulated industries rather than public interest

Citizens experiencing these failures face isolation, lack of standing, and exhaustion of remedies. This instrument provides **outside accountability as a failsafe** when domestic mechanisms fail.

## Key Mechanisms

### 1. Collective Defense for Sovereignty
Adapts NATO Article 5: "An armed attack against one or more [parties] shall be considered an attack against them all" â†’ **Systematic sovereignty degradation affecting any citizens threatens the sovereignty of all citizens**

### 2. Threshold Documentation
Integrates with **Registry of Distributed Grievance** to establish invocation thresholds:
- Pattern evidence (not isolated incidents)
- Statistical proof of systematic failures
- Multiple independent verification sources
- Standardized documentation formats

### 3. Outside Accountability Actions
Responding Signatories provide:
- Independent monitoring and shadow reporting
- Advocacy and amplification of domestic demands
- Technical assistance for accountability efforts
- Documentation preservation resistant to suppression
- Diplomatic/political pressure (where applicable)

### 4. Distributed Invocation
Any Signatory can invoke assistance for any affected parties:
- No prior association required
- No central approval needed
- No consensus required
- Multiple independent invocations permitted

### 5. Ethical Constraints
- Exhaustion of domestic remedies required
- No creation of new harms
- Truthfulness and accuracy standards
- Proportionality requirements
- Sunset upon institutional restoration

## Theoretical Significance

### 1. Inverts Traditional Sovereignty Logic
Outside intervention typically conceived as sovereignty violation. This Compact frames outside accountability as sovereignty *defense* against institutional capture.

### 2. Formalizes Transnational Advocacy
Scholars (Keck & Sikkink) documented how advocacy networks provide "boomerang effect" accountability. This Compact standardizes and formalizes these network operations.

### 3. Creates Ongoing TRC Infrastructure
Truth and Reconciliation Commissions demonstrate power of aggregated testimony. This Compact creates similar infrastructure operative *before* conflicts resolve.

### 4. Addresses International Mechanism Gaps
Complements UN human rights mechanisms, regional courts, and treaty bodies by:
- Enabling citizen-initiated accountability without state consent
- Providing rapid response through distributed activation
- Preserving comprehensive documentation for official mechanisms
- Reducing resource constraints through distributed contributions

## Ethical Safeguards

### Against Weaponization
- **Good-faith domestic engagement required**: Exhaustion of remedies prevents premature invocation
- **Truthfulness standards**: False invocations undermine legitimacy
- **Proportionality**: Actions must match documented failures
- **Non-selectivity**: Standards apply equally regardless of political alignment
- **No new harms**: Actions creating additional degradation violate Compact

### Against Paternalism
- **Support, not supplant**: Outside accountability supports domestic efforts
- **Affected parties as primary authorities**: Defer to those experiencing degradation
- **Sunset obligations**: Transfer to domestic accountability when restored
- **Transparency**: Public invocations and documentation

### Against Overreach
- **No enforcement power**: Only documentation, advocacy, monitoring
- **No coercion**: Moral and reputational pressure, not sanctions
- **Jurisdictional respect**: Operate within domestic legal frameworks
- **Resource proportionality**: Assistance proportionate to Signatory capacity
